Analyzing Obesity: Lifestyle Factors and BMI

Introduction

Obesity is a pressing global health concern, associated with chronic diseases like diabetes, cardiovascular disorders, and hypertension. Tackling obesity requires a clear understanding of the various factors that influence it—ranging from biological predispositions to everyday lifestyle choices. Our project explores the correlation between obesity levels and different health-related attributes using interactive visualizations. By identifying key physiological and behavioral patterns within the data, we aim to inform both public health policy and individual decision-making.

Dataset Overview

The dataset contains 2,111 observations collected from individuals in Mexico, Peru, and Colombia. It includes 17 features related to demographic background, health behaviors, and physical conditions. The data was obtained from a public repository as part of a research study titled Estimation of Obesity Levels Based on Eating Habits and Physical Condition.

In our project, we primarily refer to the target variable NObeyesdad, which classifies individuals into categories such as Insufficient Weight, Normal Weight, Overweight, and three levels of Obesity. For simplicity, we introduce a shortened version called Short_Weight_Type, grouping them into four broader categories: Insufficient, Normal, Overweight, and Obese.

We organize the key features into the following groups:

Physiological Features — attributes that describe a person’s physical state or biological background:

  • Age – age of the individual
  • Gender – male or female
  • Height and Weight – used to calculate BMI (Body Mass Index)
  • family_history_with_overweight – whether the person has a family history of obesity

Behavioral Features — indicators related to diet, activity, and lifestyle choices:

  • FAVC – frequent consumption of high-calorie food
  • FCVC – frequency of vegetable consumption
  • NCP – number of main meals per day
  • CAEC – frequency of snacking between meals
  • CH2O – daily water intake
  • FAF – frequency of physical activity
  • TUE – time spent using technology (screen time)
  • CALC – alcohol consumption
  • SMOKE – smoking habits
  • MTRANS – primary method of transportation
